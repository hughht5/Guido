<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/screen-shot-2012-10-15-at-13-47-09.png?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="Screen Shot 2012-10-15 at 13.47.09" title="Screen Shot 2012-10-15 at 13.47.09" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>The <a target="_blank" href="http://launch48.com/">Launch48</a> event for potential startups to get together and each build a completely new online or app business in just 48 hours is back in London this weekend, with space for 100 budding entrepreneurs to take part in the intensive caffeine-fuelled event, at <a target="_blank" href="http://www.ucl.ac.uk/">University College London</a> &#8212; kicking off on the evening of Friday October 19.</p>
<p>As well as knuckling down to some serious coding during the event &#8212; and consuming plenty of pizza and beer &#8212; participants can expect advice and support from a team of mentors &#8212; including serial entrepreneur <a target="_blank" href="http://www.linkedin.com/profile/view?id=41211115&amp;authType=NAME_SEARCH&amp;authToken=8j4R&amp;locale=en_US&amp;srchid=7b54d247-95d0-4f6a-a0ee-0c94b42e781e-0&amp;srchindex=3&amp;srchtotal=4&amp;goback=%2Efps_PBCK_%22hugh+chappell%22_*1_*1_*1_*1_*1_*1_*2_*1_Y_*1_*1_*1_false_1_R_*1_*51_*1_*51_true_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2_*2&amp;pvs=ps&amp;trk=pp_profile_name_link">Hugh Chappell</a>, investment specialist Matthew Stafford from Pembridge Partnership Limited and user experience guru Karl Sayor.</p>
<p>The last Launch48 event held in London took place in February. Since then the event has been acquired by the <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/08/06/could-birmingham-become-britains-berlin-for-tech/">Birmingham-based</a> <a target="_blank" href="http://www.linkedin.com/pub/mark-hales/17/514/745">Mark Hales</a>-owned <a target="_blank" href="http://www.oxygenaccelerator.com/">Oxygen Accelerator</a>, with Hales taking the role of Launch48 programme director.</p>
<p>&#8220;The weekend is designed to provide maximum business support for innovative ideas. During an intensive 48 hours attendees will collaborate and work to materialise their ideas into a functional business,&#8221; Hales noted in a statement. &#8221;The programme also attracts accelerators and angel investors, who are always on the lookout for the next stellar business idea.”</p>
<p>Tickets to attend the event can be bought via <a target="_blank" href="http://www.launch48.com/">www.launch48.com</a>. The event runs over three days, kicking off at 18:00 (BST) on Friday 19 October to 21:00 (BST) on Sunday October 21.</p>
<p>Startup ideas to emerge from previous Launch48 events include <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2010/09/30/from-pitch-to-prototype-londons-launch48-produces-four-potential-startups/">Matchfounders.com, Commutably and Buqoo</a>.</p>
