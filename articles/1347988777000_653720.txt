<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2010/07/github-logo.png?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="github-logo" title="github-logo" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p><a target="_blank" href="http://www.personalweb.com/">PersonalWeb</a> and <a target="_blank" href="http://www.level3.com/">Level 3 Communications</a> have filed patent complaint against Rackspace for hosting GitHub. <strong>Update:</strong> PersonalWeb has a history of suing large tech companies, including Google, Facebook and Apple.</p>
<p>PersonalWeb is the main plaintiff in the case. Level3 and Personal Web purchased several patents from Kinetech and Digital Island in 2000 and each own a 50% undivided interest in the patents. <strong>Update:</strong> According to the complaint, it&#8217;s being brought about solely by PersonalWeb, and Level 3 may not have even known that PersonalWeb was filing the complaint.</p>
<p>According to <a target="_blank" href="http://www.techdirt.com/articles/20111219/02381817122/ex-morpheus-kazaa-execs-team-up-to-become-patent-trolls.shtml">TechDirt</a>, PersonalWeb was founded by Kevin Bermeister, whose previous company Brilliant Digital Entertainment acquired the file sharing system Kazaa, and former Streamcast CEO Michael Weiss. Last year <a target="_blank" href="http://www.crn.com/news/cloud/232300530/personalweb-files-patent-lawsuits-against-top-storage-cloud-vendors.htm?cid=rssFeed">PersonalWeb began suing major tech companies</a>: Amazon, Caringo, Dropbox, EMC, Google, NEC, NetApp, VMware and YouTube. As noted by a commenter, complaints have also been filed against <a target="_blank" href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/106190872/PersonalWeb-Technologies-et-al-v-Nexsan-Technologies">Nexsan</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/106190829/PersonalWeb-Technologies-et-al-v-Yahoo">Yahoo</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/106198847/PersonalWeb-Technologies-et-al-v-Microsoft">Microsoft</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/106194754/PersonalWeb-Technologies-et-al-v-Facebook">Facebook</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/106194723/PersonalWeb-Technologies-et-al-v-Apple">Apple</a> and <a target="_blank" href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/106194748/PersonalWeb-Technologies-et-al-v-International-Business-Machines">IBM</a>.</p>
<p>PersonalWeb is registered in East Texas, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nytimes.com/2006/09/24/business/24ward.html?_r=1&amp;pagewanted=all">an infamous haven for patent lawsuits</a>.</p>
<p>When it&#8217;s not busy filing lawsuits, the company develops <a target="_blank" href="https://www.studypods.com/">StudyPods</a> a &#8220;social learning platform&#8221; that enables students to upload notes and collaborate. Students can upload assignments to StudyPod and the app will search for related notes and documents to help the student with their research. To do this it uses <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_processing">natural language processing</a>, and the company cites many patents that it holds in this area. The PersonalWeb site says the company is also applying these patents to the enterprise. The complaint also lists a PersonalWeb product called Global File Registry, a digital content management system for copyright protection.</p>
<p>Although the complaint is focused on technology in use at GitHub, the complaint targets Rackspace, GitHub&#8217;s host. &#8220;Rackspace further contributes to and induces others to manufacture, use, sell, import,and/or offer for sale these infringing products and services,&#8221; it reads.</p>
<p>What kind of precedent would it set if companies were found to be infringing just by hosting other companies&#8217; applications?</p>
