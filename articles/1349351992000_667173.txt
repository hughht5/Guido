<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/acer-iconia-w700-with-keyboard-angled.jpeg?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="Acer Iconia W700 with keyboard angled" title="Acer Iconia W700 with keyboard angled" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>Today,Â <a target="_blank" href="http://www.acer.com">Acer</a> officially unveiled the U.S. and Canadian availability, pricing and full spec list of its Iconia W700 Windows 8 tablet, which comes in three variants ranging in price from $799 ($749 in Canada) to $999, depending on hardware configuration. The tablets will ship on October 26, the same day Windows 8 is scheduled for its public release, which should provide us with an early look at how third-party offerings will perform in a market that&#8217;s yet to really be proven.</p>
<p>Acer&#8217;s Windows 8 tablets are designed to be full-powered computers in addition to portable devices, each boasting the ability to power three independent displays, and shipping with a cradle designed to hold the tablet at a variety of angles and orientations. They all come with the standard load-out of sensors, including an electronic compass, gyroscope and accelerometer, and manage a cold boot in just six seconds, according to Acer. A Bluetooth keyboard will make things easier for those who don&#8217;t like touchscreen typing, and there&#8217;s a Micro-HDMI to VGA adapter and protective case in the box.</p>

<p>Acer is also selling the W700 for the corporate market, backed by an extended two-year warranty and boasting Windows 8 Pro, with a price tag of $1,049.99. As the second largest PC maker in the U.S., it&#8217;ll be interesting to see how the company fares with its debut approach to the full-powered Windows 8 tablet market.
