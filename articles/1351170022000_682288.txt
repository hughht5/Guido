<p>Earlier this month, I had the good fortune to attend <a target="_blank" href="http://www.dld-conference.com/news/dld-events-related/dld-bosporus-one-night-in-istanbul_aid_3380.html">DLD Bosphorus</a>, an event held in Istanbul and featuring some of the hottest Turkish start-ups and entrepreneurs. It was a memorable evening. Held on the banks of the Bosphorus, at Istanbul&#8217;s amazing <a target="_blank" href="http://www.istanbulmodern.org/">Modern Museum of Art</a>, the DLD event featured a number of leading Turkish entrepreneurs including <a target="_blank" href="http://www.dld-conference.com/speakers/digital-business/sina-afra_aid_2951.html">Sina Afra</a>, the CEO of the private shopping club <a target="_blank" href="http://www.crunchbase.com/company/markafoni">Markafoni</a> and <a target="_blank" href="http://www.crunchbase.com/person/sureyya-ciliv">Sureyya Ciliv</a>, the CEO of the dominant Turkish cellphone provider <a target="_blank" href="http://www.turkcell.com.tr/Sayfalar/anasayfa.aspx">Turkcell</a>. And after dinner, I was lucky enough to interview both Afra and Ciliv about the local tech scene in Turkey. So what&#8217;s really happening on the banks of the Bosphorus? I asked two of Turkey&#8217;s most successful entrepreneurs. Their answers might convince some startup entrepreneurs to head east &#8211; to a place that, literally, bridges Europe with Asia.</p>
