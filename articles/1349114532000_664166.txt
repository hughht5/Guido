<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/google-politics-and-elections.jpg?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="Google politics and elections" title="Google politics and elections" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>For the first time ever, YouTube will offer a live video stream of the U.S. presidential and vice presidential debates this year. To do this, YouTube has <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube-global.blogspot.com/2012/10/the-2012-presidential-and-vice.html">partnered</a> with ABC News and the debates will stream on <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/abcnews">ABC News&#8217; YouTube channel</a> and YouTube&#8217;s <a target="_blank" href="http://www.youtube.com/politics?feature=inp-bp-ype-53">Election Hub</a>. The four debates, which will start on October 3 at 9pm ET, will be available for YouTube viewers around the world. In addition to ABC News, YouTube&#8217;s election page will also feature commentary and analysis from seven other partners: <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/aljazeeraenglish">Al Jazeera English</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/buzzfeed">BuzzFeed</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/oratv">Larry King</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/nytimes">New York Times</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/sourcefed">Phil DeFranco</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/univisionnoticias">Univision</a> and the <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube.com/wsjdigitalnetwork">Wall Street Journal</a>. Univision, Google notes, will also offer a live translated version of the debates on its channel.</p>
<p>YouTube&#8217;s election hub, which is only one part of Google&#8217;s larger set of <a target="_blank" href="http://www.google.com/elections/ed/us">election-focused tools</a>, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/08/22/youtube-elections-hub-2012_n_1820674.html">launched</a> in August, just ahead of the Republican and Democratic conventions. During the last primary season, YouTube &#8211; <a target="_blank" href="http://politicalticker.blogs.cnn.com/2007/06/14/the-skinny-on-cnn-youtubes-presidential-debates/">in partnership with CNN</a> &#8211; also hosted its own debates and has regularly featured <a target="_blank" href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/05/18/youtube-town-hall-politics-debates_n_863940.html">town hall-like events</a> with individual politicians ever since.</p>
<p>Here is the full schedule for the upcoming debates:</p>
<ul>
<li>October 3 at 9:00 p.m. ET (Presidential)</li>
<li>October 11 at 9:00 p.m. ET (Vice Presidential)</li>
<li>October 16 at 9:00 p.m. ET (Presidential)</li>
<li>October 22 at 9:00 p.m. ET (Presidential)</li>
</ul>
