<p>Intimidating voters at the polls is so 19th century. And, <a target="_blank" href="http://articles.latimes.com/2012/oct/08/nation/la-na-voting-laws-20121008">now that courts continue to overturn Republican voter ID laws</a>, based on the fact that just <a target="_blank" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/election-day-impersonation-an-impetus-for-voter-id-laws-a-rarity-data-show/2012/08/11/7002911e-df20-11e1-a19c-fcfa365396c8_story.html">10 incidences of voter fraud in the last decade</a> doesn&#8217;t justify disenfranchising thousands of minority voters without a driver&#8217;s license, what is the GOP to do? Thankfully, America&#8217;s #1 fake news site <a target="_blank" href="http://gizmodo.com/5954599/the-onion-shows-off-the-gops-hot-new-voter-suppression-app">has reimagined voter suppression for the 21st century</a>, with a smartphone app that automatically applies time-tested voter intimidation to dark-skinned voters. Check out their concept video at the end of this post.</p>
