<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/screen-shot-2012-09-06-at-3-46-07-pm.png?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="Kindle Paperwhite" title="Kindle Paperwhite" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>Amazon had another <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/10/11/amazon-releases-kindles-lending-library-in-uk-germany-france-with-200000-books-increases-kdp-royalty-fund/">European</a> announcement today. The <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/09/30/paperwhite-kindle-review/">Kindle Paperwhite</a> is now available to pre-order in the U.K., Germany, France, Italy and Spain. Canada won&#8217;t get it for now.</p>
<p>Both the 3G and Wi-Fi only models are available in those countries. The Wi-Fi variant costs €129 or £109 while the 3G version costs €189 or £169. As special offers are not available in Europe, the Kindle Paperwhite will come without ads.</p>
<p>British customers should expect their device sooner than other Europeans as shipments will begin on October 25 in the U.K. and on November 22 elsewhere.</p>
