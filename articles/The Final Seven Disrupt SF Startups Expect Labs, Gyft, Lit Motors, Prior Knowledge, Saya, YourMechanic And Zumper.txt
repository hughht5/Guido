<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/7974629578_b4fb00fec7_z.jpg?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="7974629578_b4fb00fec7_z" title="7974629578_b4fb00fec7_z" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>This year&#8217;s <a href="http://techcrunch.com/events/disrupt-sf-2012/event-info/">TechCrunch Disrupt San Francisco</a> has been the biggest yet. Nearly 3,000 people poured in to watch a wonderful keynote by top product CEO Jack Dorsey, talks with legends like Reid Hoffman and Ben Horowitz, and they were standing in the aisles during Michael Arrington&#8217;s historic post-IPO interview with Facebook&#8217;s Mark Zuckerberg today.</p>
<p>Tomorrow, we end on a high note with the Startup Battlefield grand finale&#8230;.</p>
<p>But before that, in addition to a panel on all things Apple with some of the world&#8217;s most vociferous Apple experts, we&#8217;ve got talks from Biz Stone, Evan Williams and Vinod Khosla planned for the day.</p>
<p>For the battlefield, we&#8217;ve narrowed down the 30 companies in our startup battlefield to seven startups competing for the grand prize: the Disrupt Cup and a $50,000 stipend.</p>
<p>Finals judges Michael Arrington (CrunchFund), Roelof Botha (Sequoia Capital), Chris Dixon (Founder Collective), David Lee (SV Angel), Marissa Mayer (Yahoo!) and David Sacks (Yammer) will have the daunting task of picking a winner from the worthy.</p>
