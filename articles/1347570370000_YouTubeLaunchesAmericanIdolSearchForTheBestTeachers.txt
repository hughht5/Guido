<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/18-youtube-for-schools.jpeg?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="18-YouTube-for-Schools" title="18-YouTube-for-Schools" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>YouTube <a target="_blank" href="http://youtube-global.blogspot.com/2012/09/finding-next-generation-of-talented.html">is looking</a> to give some much-needed celebrity status to the most engaging teachers in the world. They&#8217;re conducting an Internet-wide competition of the best video lessons, which will be judged by an all-star panel of online educators, such as <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/08/14/khan-academy-launches-the-future-of-computer-science-education/">Sal Khan</a> of <a target="_blank" href="http://www.crunchbase.com/company/khan-academy">Khan Academy</a>. &#8220;The rise of online educational videos is giving learners access to the world’s greatest thinkers and teachers, leveling the playing field for all,&#8221; explains the YouTube blog announcement. &#8220;We believe that inspiring online educators can come from all walks of life, and we want to find the next generation of educational YouTube stars.&#8221;</p>
<p>Google&#8217;s original <a target="_blank" href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/07/31/youtube-channel-original-content-200-million/">$300 million foray into</a> original content was all about unearthing the next generation of entertainment celebrities. This new education project bridges the long-held hope that the Internet can centralize the world&#8217;s best teachers with the marketing power of one of the great new media companies.</p>
