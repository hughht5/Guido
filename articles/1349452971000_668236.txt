<p>We should all thank Twitter for a world where powerful people can say delightfully crazy things without the silly filter of public relations professional protecting their reputation. This morning, in response to a promising jobs report, Former CEO of General Electric, Jack Welch, accused President Obama of manipulating the numbers. &#8220;Unbelievable jobs numbers..these Chicago guys will do anything..can&#8217;t debate so change numbers,&#8221; he tweeted. His bold conspiracy theory <a target="_blank" href="http://thinkprogress.org/economy/2012/10/05/966381/meet-the-conservatives-who-think-todays-job-numbers-are-a-conspiracy/?mobile=nc">inspired the outer rim of conservatives</a> to come out of the woodwork to defend him, and an equally entertaining response from prominent economists&#8211;all of which we&#8217;ve posted below for your friday enjoyment.</p>
<p><strong></strong><strong>Austan Goolsbee, </strong><strong>Former Chairman of the Council of Economic Advisors, </strong>was up at bat first, quickly retorting:</p>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p>love ya jack but here you&#8217;ve lost your mind @<a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/jack_welch">jack_welch</a> Unbelievable jobs <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/search/%23s">#s</a>. the Chicago guys will do anything..can&#8217;t debate, change <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/search/%23s">#s</a> — Austan Goolsbee (@Austan_Goolsbee) <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/Austan_Goolsbee/status/254206821479362561">October 5, 2012</a></p></blockquote>
<p><strong>Reuters Financial Blogger, Felix Salmon,</strong> was a close second:</p>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p>I&#8217;ll give @<a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/jack_welch">jack_welch</a> this: he is now way ahead of @<a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/rupertmurdoch">rupertmurdoch</a> in the crazy-old-man-on-twitter league tables — felix salmon (@felixsalmon) <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/felixsalmon/status/254200670855520258">October 5, 2012</a></p></blockquote>
