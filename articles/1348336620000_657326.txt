While the method isn&#8217;t public yet, chpwn has posted screenshots of the &#8220;alt&#8221; App Store Cydia running at full iPhone 5 resolution.</p>
<blockquote class='twitter-tweet'><p>@<a href="https://twitter.com/iamramsey">iamramsey</a> It&#8217;s jailbroken, what kind of proof do you need?&mdash; <br />Grant Paul (chpwn) (@chpwn) <a href='http://twitter.com/#!/chpwn/status/249321084376195072' data-datetime='2012-09-22T01:35:53+00:00'>September 22, 2012</a></p></blockquote>
<p>Jailbreaking allows users to add &#8220;homebrew&#8221; apps to the iPhone and is a method to install pirated apps onto an activated iPhone. It also potentially allows uses to unlock their iPhones to run on different carriers.</p>
<p>What does this mean? Well, the homebrew and unlocker scene just got a little more interested in the iPhone 5, which means plenty more sales. It&#8217;s also funny that this jailbreak comes so soon after launch. <a target="_blank" href="http://www.redmondpie.com/jailbreak-ios-6-iphone-and-ipod-touch-a4-based-only-using-redsn0w-tutorial/">iOS 6 has already been jailbroken</a> but bypassing hardware specific issues are always the biggest part of the problem.</p>
