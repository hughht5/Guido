<img width="100" height="54" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/shoppertrak-logo.jpg?w=100&amp;h=54&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="shoppertrak logo" title="shoppertrak logo" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p><a target="_blank" href="http://www.shoppertrak.com">ShopperTrak</a>, a company analyzing foot traffic data for retailers, just announced that it has acquired surveillance analytics startup <a target="_blank" href="http://www.reteltechnologies.com">ReTel Technologies</a>. The financial terms of the deal were not disclosed.</p>
<p>In <a target="_blank" href="http://www.itnewsonline.com/showrwstory.php?storyid=9473">the press release</a> announcing the deal, ShopperTrak Chief Marketing Officer Ed Marcheselli described ReTel as &#8220;an ideal complement&#8221; to the company&#8217;s existing services. The startup uses human auditing to analyze in-store video surveillance footage. Once its technology is integrated, it&#8217;s supposed to give ShopperTrak customers &#8220;rapid access to customer demographics, queue analysis, speed-of-service and sales process effectiveness.&#8221;</p>
<p>ReTel was part of the TechStars incubator&#8217;s 2009 class, and it <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2009/12/02/retel-technologies-raises-1-million-for-surveillance-video-analytics/">raised $1 million in funding</a> from Jeff Clavier&#8217;s SoftTech VC, TechStars&#8217; David Cohen, and others. The San Francisco-based company will help Chicago-based ShopperTrak establish a presence in Silicon Valley. Retel co-founder and Chief Marketing Officer Adam Rodnitzsky told me the entire team will be be joining ShopperTrak as part of the deal.</p>
<p>ShopperTrak claims to count billions of shoppers in more than 50,000 locations across 74 countries.</p>
