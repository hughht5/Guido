Luckily, the Internet has sprung into action with some great resources.</p>
<p>Google has a <a target="_blank" href="http://www.google.org/crisisresponse/">fantastic program</a> that prepares the world for major events involving Mother Nature, and it has <a target="_blank" href="http://google.org/crisismap/sandy-2012">set up a crisis map</a> for people on the East Coast preparing for Hurricane Sandy.</p>
<p>Have a look:</p>
<p><a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/10/28/google-has-set-up-a-crisis-map-for-those-preparing-for-hurricane-sandy/snapshot-102812-1112-am-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-685321"></a></p>
<p>This is an extremely helpful tool, which includes weather service alerts, as well as notifications of areas that are being evacuated. If you or a loved one are going to be affected by Hurricane Sandy, then <a target="_blank" href="http://google.org/crisismap/sandy-2012">this is going to be something worth bookmarking and checking often</a>.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m currently checking the webcams in the Philly area, because that&#8217;s where my friends and family are.</p>
<p>As you know, <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/10/27/raincheck-google-cancels-nyc-android-event-planned-for-monday-due-to-hurricane-sandy/">Google has cancelled its NYC Android event</a> on Monday, and <a target="_blank" href="http://allthingsd.com/20121028/facebook-gift-event-also-gets-blown-out-of-nyc-by-hurricane-sandy/">other tech events are affected as well</a>.</p>

<p>In addition to Google&#8217;s Hurricane page, there are quite a few other resources, links and stories for you to bookmark and check out:</p>
<p>- <a target="_blank" href="http://gis.nyc.gov/oem/he/map.htm?lon=-73.99010161004679&amp;lat=40.71539402309297">A great map of the evacuated areas in NYC</a> via the city<br />
- <a target="_blank" href="http://google.org/crisismap/2012-sandy-nyc">A page specifically for the NYC area</a>, thanks to a Googler in the comments.<br />
- <a target="_blank" href="http://www.twitter.com/breakingweather">BreakingWeather on Twitter</a> is reporting bits and pieces of information, like NYC being out of bread. The Weather Channel has a <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/twc_hurricane">&#8220;Hurricane Central&#8221; account</a>.<br />
- The <a target="_blank" href="http://www.redcross.org/find-help/contact-family/register-safe-listing">Red Cross has set up a page</a> to help keep families connected as the storms roll in.<br />
- The <a target="_blank" href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/10/26/hurricane-sandy-how-to-help_n_2022488.html">Huffington Post has quite a few resources</a> listed, and are adding to the list as the day goes on.<br />
- In an attempt to keep people&#8217;s spirits up, <a target="_blank" href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/summeranne/hurricane-sandy-texts-from-your-mom">BuzzFeed is featuring texts from moms about the Hurricane</a>.<br />
- The Weather Channel is streaming <a target="_blank" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&amp;v=0XoEypA0Iko">live Hurricane Sandy coverage on YouTube</a> and have a <a target="_blank" href="http://iwitness.weather.com/">section dedicated to the storm on its iWitness site</a>.<br />
- Many elected officials in potentially affected areas are tweeting about the situation. <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/Michael_Nutter">Philadelphia&#8217;s Michael Nutter</a>, <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/CoryBooker">Newark&#8217;s Cory Booker</a>, <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/MikeBloomberg">New York City&#8217;s Mike Bloomberg</a> and <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/GovChristie">New Jersey Governor Chris Christie</a>.<br />
- <a target="_blank" href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/10/28/hurricane-sandy-shoppers_n_2035931.html?ncid=edlinkusaolp00000003">Supermarkets in NYC are getting emptied out</a>, says Huffington Post</p>
