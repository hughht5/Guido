<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/logo_trulia_large.png?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="logo_trulia_large" title="logo_trulia_large" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>Real estate listings and search site Trulia has just made its debut on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol &#8220;TRLA.&#8221; The company&#8217;s stock opened at $22.10, up 30 percent from the company&#8217;s pricing. This puts Trulia&#8217;s valuation at around $470 million. Yesterday, Trulia prices its shares at <a target="_blank" href="http://dealbook.nytimes.com/2012/09/19/trulia-prices-i-p-o-at-17-a-share-above-expected-range/">$17 per share</a>, above the expected range of $14 to $16 per share. Trulia raised $102 million in the offering. </p>
<p>Trulia, which <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/08/17/trulia-goes-after-zillows-real-estate-files-75m-ipo-now-has-22m-monthly-users/">filed to go public in July</a>, is seeing arounf 22 million monthly unique visitors, up from 5 million in June 2009. Paid subscribers — its primary source of revenue, in addition to online ads — have also grown significantly. </p>
<p>Revenues in the six months ending in June are up from last year $29 million today versus $16 million for the period a year ago ($38.5 million for all of 2011). Meanwhile, net losses are also growing: they’re now at $7.6 million ($6.2 million last year). Average revenues per user are up and are at $1,318 compared to $1,194 a year ago for the first six months of the year, or $140 versus $91 for monthly revenues.</p>
<p>Trulia has raised $32.8 million in funding from Sequoia, Accel and others. The company&#8217;s primary competitor is Zillow. Zillow <a target="_blank" href="http://thenextweb.com/insider/2012/09/13/oh-snap-zillow-sues-online-real-estate-rival-trulia-patent-court-docs/">recently sued</a> Trulia for patent infringement. </p>
<p>For basis of comparison, Zillow shares <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2011/07/20/zillow-soars-200-percent-in-first-trade-with-over-1-billion-valuation/">soared 200 percent</a> on its first trade last year. Currently, Zillow is trading at $44.72 per share, giving the company a $1.3 billion valuation.</p>
