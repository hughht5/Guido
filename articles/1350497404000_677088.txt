The online ad network is seeing 43 percent of smartphone web usage coming through iPhones up to the 4S, plus another 3 percent from the iPhone 5 alone. By contrast, the Samsung Galaxy S III is driving 2 percent of mobile web traffic on its network, combined with 15 percent across all other Samsung mobile devices.</p>
<p><a target="_blank" href="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/web-traffic-share-by-mobile-phone.png" rel="lightbox[677088]"></a>Both phones are taking up a huge percentage overall, however, with other smartphones taken together adding up to just 37 percent overall. Apple and Samsung overall account for a total of 63 percent of the mobile traffic Chitika sees through its millions of daily ad impressions. Last week, the company told us that the <a target="_blank" href="http://insights.chitika.com/2012/iphone-5-galaxy-s-iii-study/">iPhone 5 had quickly risen to surpass the GSIII</a> as a traffic driver on its network, but these latest figures prove there&#8217;s no doubt which two companies are battling it out for overall smartphone market dominance.</p>
