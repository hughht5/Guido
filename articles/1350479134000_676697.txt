<img width="100" height="44" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/screen-shot-2012-10-17-at-14-04-02.png?w=100&amp;h=44&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="Screen Shot 2012-10-17 at 14.04.02" title="Screen Shot 2012-10-17 at 14.04.02" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>Yesterday we saw the <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/10/16/handybook-secures-2m-from-highland-capital-partners-and-general-catalyst-for-its-uber-for-the-home/">funding announcement of HandyBook</a>, and it appears this whole area of booking labour fast is really starting to take off. PeoplePerHour, a sort of &#8216;European E-Lance&#8217; emerged in 2008, <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2009/04/20/peopleperhour-secures-angel-round-to-create-a-european-e-lance/">scored a seed round</a> then secured <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2010/02/17/index-ventures-invests-in-peopleperhour-as-business-flows-east-to-west/">VC round</a> from Index ventures. Now it&#8217;s announcing a further £2M ($3.2M) investment from Index, and boosting its management team with new appointments including CFO David Engel, former financial Director at Skype and Yahoo Europe.</p>
<p>In addition it&#8217;s launched it own iPhone app has gone through some significant changes, launching a new website in August with a new UI and a new product called ‘Hourlies’ which allows anyone to buy and sell pre-packaged services at a fixed price in bundles, starting from one hour. It&#8217;s a different take on the old RFP model and allows anyone to sell any kind of hour. We&#8217;l let your imagination run with that thought. That means it it&#8217;s the only such e-lance type site with a an RFP and supply driven model.</p>
<p>Hourlies launched in August and popular categories include design, writing and digital but also someone making you a custom made dog coat in three hours for £25, or someone to write your CV from scratch in one hour for £10, says the company.</p>
<p>Founder and CEO Xenios Thrasyvoulou says they want to &#8220;make buying and selling of services as easy and widespread as buying on Amazon or eBay”.</p>
<p>David Engel, formerly the FD of Skype and Yahoo! Europe has now joined as CFO and Katie Kitiri, formerly the Head of Customer Service, Trust &amp; Safety at Gumtree, joined as Director of Operations.</p>
