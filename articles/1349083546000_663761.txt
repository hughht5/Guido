<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/softbank-logo.jpeg?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="softbank-logo" title="softbank-logo" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>Japan&#8217;s third largest carrier, Softbank, is to acquire fourth-placed carrier <a target="_blank" href="http://www.eaccess.net/en/">eAccess</a> in a share swap deal valued at just under ¥200 billion ($2.6 billion), <a target="_blank" href="http://uk.reuters.com/article/2012/10/01/us-eaccess-softbank-buyout-idUKBRE89006320121001">Reuters</a> is reporting (original story at the <a target="_blank" href="http://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&amp;sl=auto&amp;tl=en&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.nikkei.com%2Farticle%2FDGXNASDD0104R_R01C12A0MM0000%2F%3Fdg%3D1&amp;anno=2">Nikkei</a> business daily).</p>
<p>The deal is aimed at acquiring customers and bulking out Softbank&#8217;s spectrum frequency holdings to support LTE services, according to the reports.</p>
<p>Softbank, whose charismatic CEO <a target="_blank" href="http://money.cnn.com/galleries/2012/technology/1203/gallery.coolest-tech-2015/7.html">Masayoshi Son is the man with the 300-year business plan</a>, will gain enough customers to put it within touching distance of Japan&#8217;s second placed carrier, KDDI. Reuters reports that Softbank and eAccess together had 34.3 million users, as of the end of August &#8212; to KDDI&#8217;s 35.9 million. First placed carrier, NTTDoCoMo has more than 60 million mobile customers.</p>
<p>Back in August <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nttdocomo.com/pr/2012/001606.html">NTTDoCoMo announced that subscribers to its LTE service Xi</a> had passed the five million mark. The service &#8212; Japan&#8217;s first LTE offering &#8212; was <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nttdocomo.com/pr/2010/001494.html">launched at the end of 2010</a>.</p>
