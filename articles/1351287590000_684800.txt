Graduate student Sung Wook Paek estimates that reflective paint-covered asteroids would increase the pressure from the Sun&#8217;s photons (light) to push a doomsday rock off course. In the 1990&#8242;s quality video below, Paek demonstrates how enormous clouds of paint lodged from cosmic paintball guns could envelop a 27-gigaton rock headed towards Earth in 2029.</p>

<p>Paek&#8217;s paper was the winner of the United Nation&#8217;s competition for novel ways of finding humanity-saving solutions to looming asteroids. New challenge: now that we&#8217;ve found a unique way to destroy asteroids, try to come up with a blockbuster movie based on suspiciously good looking painters.</p>
