<p>Today, as we check out Apple&#8217;s Q4 2012 earnings, we learned that the company did an amazing $4.2B in sales in its retail stores alone. In Q3, that number was about $4B.</p>
<p>People lining up at its retail stores for an iPhone 5 certainly helped Apple march towards an impressive quarter, and things are slowing down. The leading product line in Q4 for Apple was the iPhone, big shocker, with about $17.1B in revenue. It&#8217;s clear that the iPad also had something to do with that, as the company announced at its event this week that it has <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/10/23/tim-cook-apple-has-sold-100m-ipads-in-just-two-and-a-half-years/">sold over 100M units to date</a>. We did learn however that iPad sales have slowed down. That&#8217;ll pick up thanks to the <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/10/23/ipad-mini/">mini</a>.</p>
<p>Stepping into an Apple store is still a magical experience for a lot of people, even though I&#8217;ve personally run out of things to buy there. However, I enjoy playing around with the gadgets and watching others do the same. It&#8217;s as if they&#8217;ve stepped into Willy Wonka&#8217;s factory.</p>
<p>This is developing.</p>
