<img width="100" height="70" src="http://tctechcrunch2011.files.wordpress.com/2012/10/facebook-mobile-shift-people.png?w=100&amp;h=70&amp;crop=1" class="attachment-tc-carousel-river-thumb wp-post-image" alt="Facebook Mobile Shift People" title="Facebook Mobile Shift People" style="float: left; margin: 0 10px 7px 0;" /><p>Facebook may have made 14% of its ad revenue from mobile this quarter, but it has to keep up with shifting user habits. The amount of people who accessed Facebook solely from mobile each month grew 24% to 126 million this quarter, according to Facebook&#8217;s just-published <a target="_blank" href="http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1326801/000132680112000006/fb-9302012x10q.htm">10-Q document</a>. Meanwhile mature markets are still growing, albeit slowly. The US grew from 168 million to 171 million monthly users this quarter.</p>
<p>Facebook wrote in its 10-Q: &#8220;Approximately 126 million mobile MAUs accessed Facebook solely through mobile apps or our mobile website during the month ended September 30, 2012, increasing 24% from 102 million during the month ended June 30, 2012&#8243;</p>
